## For inverse computations

Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) have proven particularly effective in solving inverse problems within differential equations, [30](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-30) demonstrating their applicability across science, engineering, and economics. They have shown to be useful for solving inverse problems in a variety of fields, including nano-optics,[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-31) topology optimization/characterization,[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-32) [multiphase flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiphase_flow "Multiphase flow") in porous media,[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-33)[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-34) and high-speed fluid flow.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-35)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-:4-13) PINNs have demonstrated flexibility when dealing with noisy and uncertain observation datasets. They also demonstrated clear advantages in the inverse calculation of parameters for multi-fidelity datasets, meaning datasets with different quality, quantity, and types of observations. Uncertainties in calculations can be evaluated using ensemble-based or Bayesian-based calculations.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-36)[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-37)

PINNs can also be used in connection with [symbolic regression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbolic_regression "Symbolic regression") for discovering the mathematical expression in connection with discovery of parameters and functions. One example of such application is the study on chemical ageing of cellulose insulation material,[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics-informed_neural_networks#cite_note-38) in this example PINNs are used to first discover a parameter for a set of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) and later a function solution, which is later used to find a more fitting expression using a [symbolic regression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbolic_regression "Symbolic regression") with a combination of operators.
