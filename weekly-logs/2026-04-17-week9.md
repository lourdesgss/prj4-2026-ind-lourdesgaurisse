This week focused on developing a better understanding of the physical structure of the printer and how this structure was represented within the dataset. After identifying several inconsistencies and aggregation issues during the noise analysis, it became necessary to investigate how the logical data dimensions corresponded to the actual hardware layout of the machine.

To do this, I created exploratory plots to analyse the sheets and physical layout of the machine. The goal was not only to inspect statistical behaviour, but also to understand whether certain anomalies followed physical patterns across the print heads.

By visualising measurements spatially and grouping data across different hardware dimensions, I was able to clarify how nozzle ordering, chip structure, and colour separation were organised within the printer system.

In addition, the visual exploration exposed some challenges related to missing measurements and uneven distributions across chips. These findings reinforced the importance of validating the physical interpretation of the data before applying further modelling or optimisation techniques.

## Reflection

This week shifted my perspective from viewing the dataset purely as an abstract collection of features towards understanding it as a representation of a physical industrial process. Creating visual representations of the printer layout made it easier to reason about the behaviour of the system and to identify patterns that may have otherwise remained hidden.

The exploration also highlighted how easily incorrect assumptions about physical structure can affect later analyses. While this work was less focused on immediate modelling progress, it strengthened the foundation required for reliable interpretation of future results.
