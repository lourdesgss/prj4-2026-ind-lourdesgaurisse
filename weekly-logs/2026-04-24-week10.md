This week the focus was formalising the data reduction pipeline. The raw data contains multiple rows per unique parameter combination corresponding to the 6 measurement sheets, which needed to be collapsed before any modelling could begin.

The work started by verifying the structure of `dt2` across groups: a `groupby` over `Color$`, `HeadIdx#`, `V`, `F_r`, and `Coverage#`confirmed that every group has exactly one unique `dt2` value, meaning `dt2` can safely be included as a group key. A count of group sizes confirmed that the vast majority of parameter combinations (102,300 out of ~104,340) have the expected 6 rows, with a small number of incomplete groups (1–5 rows) that will not affect the mean aggregation meaningfully.

A Polars lazy pipeline was then written to group by all 6 keys and compute the column-wise mean across all 1,136 `Value_*`waveform columns. The resulting collapsed dataset contained 104,340 rows × 1,126 columns, and was saved as `collapsed_waveform_tuning.parquet`.

At this point, the primary and secondary objectives of the project were revised. This was in order to make sure the work was staying aligned with the main goal, instead of drifting into unnecessary or marginal analysis.

As a first step toward the extension model (waveform tuning per colour), the collapsed data was also split into four per-colour parquet files (`collapsed_C.parquet`, `collapsed_M.parquet`, `collapsed_Y.parquet`, `collapsed_K.parquet`) to enable parallel nozzle-level outlier analysis per colour channel.

## Reflection

This week reinforced how much of a modelling pipeline's reliability depends on decisions made before any model is trained. Verifying the group structure of `dt2` before including it as a key rather than assuming it was a small but important step that prevented potential aggregation errors downstream. 

Working at this scale also made clear that data engineering choices carry real consequences: using a lazy evaluation pipeline was a necessity given the dataset size. The result is a compact intermediate dataset that can be shared across both the core and extension modelling tracks without duplication.
