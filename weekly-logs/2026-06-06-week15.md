# Overview

This week was primarily a planning and reorientation week.

As part of the ongoing spatial analysis, a 3D representation of the printer output was produced, mapping chip position, nozzle position, and SD value to the three axes. While the visualisation confirms the spatial structure of the data, it serves more as a reference than an actionable finding for now. With time constraints in mind, the analytical path is being deprioritised in favour of moving toward a working implementation.

The focus this week shifted toward grounding the theoretical framework developed in previous weeks into something more concrete. Building on the earlier investigation into spatially-structured models, specifically SAR and CAR models explored last week as ways of capturing neighbourhood dependencies, this week extended that line of thinking toward neural approaches. CNNs were considered as a way to exploit local spatial structure through learned filters, and GNNs as a more flexible alternative that could encode irregular nozzle-to-nozzle relationships as a graph. The goal is to identify which of these directions best bridges the conceptual spatial field model with a practical, trainable architecture. An MLP baseline remains the starting point, but the investigation is building the case for what a justified next step would look like.

Initial coordination with teammates also took place to align on responsibilities and knowledge gaps ahead of the final stretch.

## Reflection

This week I focused less about exploring the data and more on implementing the ideas developed so far. The spatial field framing from previous weeks suggested that standard independent-output models may not be the most natural fit, and working through SAR/CAR and then CNN/GNN architectures has been useful for understanding what assumptions each approach makes about the structure of the output space.
With time constraints becoming more real, the priority is now a working end-to-end pipeline. The analytical path isn't abandoned, but the immediate goal is an MVP that can be evaluated.
