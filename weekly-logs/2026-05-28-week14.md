
# Week 14

During this week, I delved deeper into formalising the function definition, which required determining appropriate encoding strategies (particularly for variables that are not clearly discrete or continuous), defining per-nozzle and per-chip configurations, and structuring the system through a [formal mathematical definition](../evidence/physical-notes/2-dimensionality-definition.pdf).

I remained in contact with the Canon coach via email and attended the weekly meeting, where I presented an alternative perspective on the modelling problem. Rather than treating the printer response as a matrix of independent nozzle values, I proposed viewing it as a continuous spatial response field. This perspective was motivated by the observation that neighbouring nozzles influence one another, making local spatial relationships an important aspect of the system.

Modelling the response as a continuous field could enable the use of spatial kernels to capture neighbourhood effects more naturally and may reduce the need to manually define coupling weights between nozzles. This remains an exploratory direction, but it provides a useful conceptual framework for future modelling efforts.

## Reflection

This week marked a shift from viewing the system as a collection of independent nozzle measurements towards considering it as a spatially structured response field. Initially, I approached the problem from a discrete perspective, where each nozzle value would be modelled individually. However, further analysis highlighted the strong spatial relationships between neighbouring nozzles and the difficulty of defining coupling effects through manually assigned weights.

Exploring a continuous representation of the response field provided an alternative perspective that may better capture these local dependencies. Although this idea remains exploratory, it helped clarify several modelling decisions and revealed potential directions for future experimentation, especially since this approach hasn't been previously considered by Canon for this problem's approach/ 

The process of formalising the function also exposed a number of practical challenges regarding dimensionality, parameter encoding, and the distinction between continuous and discrete variables. Working through these definitions improved my understanding of the system's structure and highlighted the importance of establishing precise mathematical foundations before proceeding with implementation.
