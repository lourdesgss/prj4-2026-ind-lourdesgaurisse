# Data Dictionary
tags: #analysis

- [Offset Printing](https://global.canon/en/technology/iqq-evaluation-2022.html): analog method involving the transfer of ink from a metal printing plate to the printing surface via a rubber blanket. It achieves consistently high quality printing, making it well suited for high-volume prints of the same content.

- [Digital Printing](https://global.canon/en/technology/iqq-evaluation-2022.html): an image based in digital data is printed directly onto the printing surface. As printing plates are not required, this method is more suitable for variable printing, where on-sheet content may differ for every page, and print-on-demand (POD) printing, which produces fewer copies per round.

- [Inkjet Printing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inkjet_printing): type of computer printing that recreates a digital image by propelling fine droplets of ink onto various substrates. This is a type of digital printing.

- [Laser Printing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_printing):
electrostatic digital printing process that uses a repeatedly passing laser beam back and forth over a negatively charged cylinder (a "drum") to define a differently charged image. The drum then selectively collects electrically charged powdered ink and transfers the image to paper, which is then heated to fuse the ink to the paper. This is a type of digital printing.

- [Print On Demand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Print_on_demand): printing technology and business process in which copies are not printed until the company receives an order, allowing prints in single or small quantities.

- [Image Quality Qualitative (IQQ) Evaluation Technology](https://global.canon/en/technology/iqq-evaluation-2022.html): system that allows to assign a value to subjective image quality criteria. It is composed of five evaluation metrics:

-[Banding](https://prisco.com/inkjet-banding-what-is-it-and-how-can-it-be-fixed): Banding is a defect in inkjet printing that will appear as horizontal or vertical lines in the printed output. This can be caused by blocked nozzles, deflected nozzles, or a misaligned printhead.

- [Waveform](https://imagexpert.com/what-is-a-waveform/): the electrical signal that is sent to each nozzle on the printhead that causes each individual drop to be fired.
- [PZT (Piezoelectric ceramic material)](https://imagexpert.com/what-is-a-waveform/): a type of piezoelectric material that can generate electrical signals in response to mechanical stress and can undergo changes in size when subjected to an electric field
- [Resonance](https://imagexpert.com/what-is-a-waveform/): interaction of pressure waves inside the ink chamber, where waves from one pulse can reinforce or interfere with waves from previous pulses
- [Crosstalk](https://imagexpert.com/what-is-a-waveform/): unintended interference between printheads, nozzles, and droplets when printing
